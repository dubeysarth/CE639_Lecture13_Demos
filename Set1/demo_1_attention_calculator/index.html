Scaled Dot-Product Attention Calculator

See exactly how Q, K, and V matrices produce attention weights. Click any cell to edit its value, then watch each step of the pipeline recompute instantly. The demo uses a 4-day streamflow window encoding precipitation (P), potential evapotranspiration (PET), and soil wetness fraction (SWF).

Hydrological Setup
Each row of Q, K, V represents one time step (Day 1–4). The three columns encode hydro-meteorological features. Day 4 is the query — the model asks: "which past days are most relevant for forecasting today's discharge?"

Try 1: High SWF Day

Set Day 1 SWF (column 2) to 0.9 and Day 4 SWF to 0.9. Watch Day 4's query strongly attend to Day 1 — similar soil-moisture states are linked.

Try 2: Remove Scaling

Toggle Unscaled. The raw dot products are large, so softmax saturates — one weight dominates and the others collapse near zero.

Try 3: Bahdanau Mode

Switch to Bahdanau. Additive attention uses a learned vector v to score each alignment — notice how the weights differ from dot-product.
