Transformer Encoder–Decoder for 7-Day Streamflow Forecasting

This walkthrough follows one deterministic forecasting problem: 30 days of hydro-meteorological history enter the encoder, and the decoder produces a 7-day streamflow forecast. Step through the pipeline to see where embeddings, positional encoding, masking, self-attention, cross-attention, and output projection enter the story.

Why this architecture mattered

Recurrent models compress long hydrological histories into a narrow hidden-state bottleneck. The original Transformer replaced that serial bottleneck with direct token-to-token attention, making long-range rainfall and wetness dependencies easier to access.

But attention alone is not enough. The model still needs positional encoding to know which storm came first, and the decoder still needs a causal mask so forecast day 3 cannot peek at forecast day 6 during training.

Teacher forcing vs autoregressive generation

          
One concept, two operating modes.

        

        

          

            
Training mode

            

              The decoder receives the shifted target sequence in parallel. A causal mask blocks illegal future lookups,
              but all seven positions are processed in one batched pass.
            

          

          

            
Inference mode

            

              The decoder has only what it has already generated. Forecast day 1 can use
              source memory plus generated target history up to that step, so uncertainty can accumulate across the
              horizon.
